# ADR-0002: Two-Tier Risk Checking (Signal vs Account)

**Status:** Accepted  
**Date:** 2025-02-01  
**Deciders:** Olabode Olaleye

## Context

Trade signals are generated by strategy evaluators and enriched by ML models. Before becoming orders, they must be validated for both intrinsic quality and account-level safety. Originally, a single `RiskChecker` handled everything — signal sanity checks (is the SL above entry for a buy?), account checks (is there margin?), and portfolio checks (is there correlation?). This created a coupling problem: approved signals were permanently consumed, so if an account-level check failed (insufficient margin), the signal was wasted — no other account could use it.

## Decision

Split risk checking into two independent tiers:

**Tier 1 (`ISignalValidator`)** — Signal-level validation. Runs in `SignalOrderBridgeWorker` immediately when a signal is created. Checks intrinsic quality only: entry price positivity, SL/TP directional consistency, minimum SL distance, risk-reward ratio, mandatory stop-loss, ML agreement, signal expiry. Approves or rejects the signal. Account state is never referenced.

**Tier 2 (`IRiskChecker`)** — Account-level validation. Runs when a specific trading account attempts to create an order from an approved signal via `POST /order/from-signal`. Checks margin availability, exposure limits, position counts, drawdown gates, spread validation, lot constraints, correlation limits, consecutive loss streaks. Failure does NOT reject the signal — it records a `SignalAccountAttempt` failure, leaving the signal available for other accounts.

## Alternatives Considered

**Single-pass validation with signal reservation.** The signal would be "reserved" for an account during validation, then released if the account check failed. Rejected because reservation introduces distributed locking complexity and TTL management for reserved signals.

**Account-unaware signals with post-order risk checks.** Let the order be created, then cancel it if risk checks fail. Rejected because creating and cancelling orders generates noise in the audit trail and confuses the EA's order tracking.

## Consequences

**Positive:**
- Approved signals remain consumable by multiple accounts until they expire, enabling multi-account trading from the same signal source.
- Tier 1 runs asynchronously (event-driven), keeping signal approval fast. Tier 2 runs synchronously in the order creation request, giving the caller immediate feedback.
- Each tier can evolve independently. Adding a new signal quality check doesn't affect account risk logic, and vice versa.

**Negative:**
- Two validation passes means two code paths to maintain. A change to "what constitutes a valid signal" must be made in the correct tier — putting an account-specific check in Tier 1 would break multi-account support.
- Tier 2 failure reasons must be clear enough that the caller (EA or dashboard) can distinguish "signal is bad" (don't retry) from "this account can't trade it right now" (try another account or wait).
